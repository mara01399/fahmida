I could use sound effects and music as well as both diegetic and non-diegetic sounds as long as they are neither dialog or narration. In the segment the task was individual.

The requirement of making the video was, it must be recorded in my mobile phone and edited in Adobe Premiere Pro. I have never use premier pro before. So it was a kind of challange for me.

Pre-production part 1 - Treatment

The next assignment was a group work. I was in a fantastic group of my five classmates. In this assignment we had to make a treatment plan of our preproduction with the following content.

  • A title- Is supposed to create interest and curiosity.
  • A theme- An emotion and a working line for the crew.
  • A logline- Present the protagonist, the antagonist, the conflict. In order to know, who is doing what and what is the opposition.
  • The treatment- And finally we had to write the entire story.

Pre-production part 2 - Storyboard

Our story board is the entire video shot by shot with all relevant information for us to create the video and for a production manager to test our video before production. In our storyboard we must describe shots and angles etc. The storyboard must be clear how the shot is framed. If our subject or camera is moving, we must write that explicitly. The storyboard must also include a dramaturgical curve, where we show the rise and fall of intensity in our video.

The storyboard must include the following information:

  • Location and time
  • Framing(close up,middle shot..)
  • Angle if different from normal
  • Camera movements if any (zoom, pan…)
  • Any transitions
  • Important sounds
  • A short description in words if necessary
  • Props, clothes, light and anything else essential.

Customer pitch

In this assignment we had to prepare a pitch that is a combination of the presentation format Pecha Kucha and showing our end product.

The Pecha Kucha slides must include the following:

  • Our website must be included, somehow, as a part of our presentation.
  • Our visual design must be demonstrated and included in the pitch.
  • Have the client brief’s needs, goals and wishes for the solutions in mind.
  • Use knowledge from personas and the target audience to support decisions.
  • We had to make our pitch informative, to-the-point and a convincing story of our work.
