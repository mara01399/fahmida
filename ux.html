Basic UX

Second theme was all about to User experience. It was a team work. We had to work in groups. We learned working in programs like Adobe XD, Illustrator and reaserch method like Five Second Test. Using these tools, we learned how to work with data collections, prototyping and communicating with the stakeholders.

During the theme of UX I practised my skills at four different kinds of research methods for collecting data followed by an analysis of the data collected and a representation of both my research and insights from those data.

Tasks included in this assignment was:

  • Desk research
  • Observation research
  • Interview research
  • Survey research
  • Present research and analysis insights

It was an indidual work on the first week of the theme. I had to choose one of the three topics. Topics are given below. And I chose to go with T-shirt Production.

  • T-shirt production
  • Online shops selling t-shirts
  • T-shirt trends amongst customers

I had to collect data on the tasks method that has been given before. And here what I gathered from my research.

First group work:

The second module's assignment was a group work and focuses on getting from first ideas to Early Prototypes for the concept of a small webshop selling t-shirts. Informed by my research, we had to find out what sort of a t-shirt webshop we will be making and who our target audience are.

We made a very good group to work with. They were so helpfull during the task.

The prototype must include 10 different items (t-shirts) or more. Our early prototypes must be designed in Adobe XD and made either as a website or app. The focus is on working with UI and UX conventions, design patterns and UI kits, and also, we have to produce relevant content. A required part of our design is the purchase flow for users interested in buying an item after having visited our webshop. Our XD-prototype must be testable on mobile or computer for testing Thursday next week. We were to find or create pictures and text in accord with the type of t-shirt webshop we create as a solution.

The prototype must be designed for:

The target audience user’s goal in visiting our webshop is as a minimum to buy a product that we are selling that they find meets their needs and preferences.

The user found the site to learn more about the product and possibly buy it from us. The user searched online (text or voice query) for the product and has now ended up on a specific page of our webshop (can be the homepage, but might not be).

The goal of the business is, of course, to sell a product to the customer that is the right product for the customer to avoid having to receive return goods or other trouble.

Now it´s time to come up with a solution for the user.

So far, we’ve worked on deciding on ideas developing early prototypes if our concept and testing our t-shirt webshop and concept selling a specific type of product to a specific type of target audience defined in detail by us. Hopefully, we also produced some insights and decided what to improve in the next and final iteration of our solution!

Now, our job is to finalise our XD prototype as a proof-of-concept solution prototype and document the solution in a visually sharp and communicative format. In descriptions, footnotes and references make it clear how our solution can be traced back into test and research insights as well as in UX design principles and practices!

Now it´s time to prepare an amazinly convincing proof-of-concept pitch of our solution to our XD problems.

Here we have to present ourselves and our agenda. We have to make our concept very clear like what the problem and what is our unique selling propositon. We have to tell the story behind in order to convince the client.we have to be very clear about what we are presenting.

In our pitch we have to include the following informations.

  • Our UX team
  • Our UX solution
  • Our UX problem(s)
  • Our UX target users
  • Our UX goal with the solution
  • Our UX solution’s value to users and key players
  • Our UX key insights from research and user tests
  • Our UX design process of creating your webshop
  • Our UX next step (the future of this, next iteration improvements).
